# Ethical Theories – Case Studies and Verdicts

## 1. Consequentialism (العواقبية)

**Core Idea:** An action is right if its consequences produce more good than harm.

### Case: AI Medical Diagnosis Without Consent
A hospital uses patient data without consent to train an AI that successfully detects cancer earlier and saves thousands of lives.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** The overall benefits (saved lives) outweigh the privacy harms.

### Case: Cambridge Analytica Data Scandal
Personal Facebook data is used to influence elections without users' knowledge.

**Verdict:** Guilty ❌

**Reason:** The harm to privacy and public trust outweighs any benefits.

---

## 2. Egoism (الأنانية)

**Core Idea:** An action is right if it maximizes the actor's own interests.

### Case: Company Sells User Data for Profit
A company secretly sells customer data to increase revenue.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** The company benefits financially.

### Case: Employee Steals Trade Secrets for Personal Gain
An employee steals proprietary information to start a competing business.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** The employee's own interests are advanced.

---

## 3. Utilitarianism (النفعية)

**Core Idea:** The morally right action produces the greatest good for the greatest number.

### Case: Trolley Problem
A train will kill 5 people unless diverted to a track where it will kill 1 person.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** Saving 5 lives is better than losing 1.

### Case: Mass Vaccination Program
A vaccine causes minor side effects in a few people but protects millions.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** Society gains a much larger benefit overall.

---

## 4. Altruism (الإيثار)

**Core Idea:** Actions should benefit others, even at personal cost.

### Case: Donating a Kidney to a Stranger
A healthy person donates a kidney to save someone else's life.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** The action prioritizes another person's welfare.

### Case: Open-Source Security Research
A researcher releases security tools freely rather than selling them.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** Others benefit more than the actor.

---

## 5. Deontology (نظرية الواجب)

**Core Idea:** Actions are judged by duties, rules, and moral obligations.

### Case: Ethical Hacking
A security tester hacks a company system with permission to find vulnerabilities.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** The action follows professional duties and authorization.

### Case: Unauthorized Hacking
A hacker accesses company systems without permission.

**Verdict:** Guilty ❌

**Reason:** It violates rules and duties regardless of outcomes.

---

## 6. Human Nature Theory (الطبيعة البشرية)

**Core Idea:** Moral responsibility depends on human capabilities such as reason and free will.

### Case: Insanity Defense
A person commits a crime while suffering severe mental illness and cannot understand their actions.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** The person lacks full rational capacity.

### Case: Unqualified Data Manager
A company allows someone with no cybersecurity knowledge to manage sensitive data.

**Verdict:** Guilty ❌

**Reason:** The decision ignores human limitations and capabilities.

---

## 7. Relativism (النسبية)

**Core Idea:** Right and wrong depend on cultural and social norms.

### Case: Parents Monitoring Children's Phones
Parents access their child's phone and messages.

**Verdict:** Depends ⚖️

**Saudi Arabia:** Often viewed as acceptable.

**United States:** Often viewed as a privacy concern.

### Case: Workplace Surveillance
Employers monitor employee activity.

**Verdict:** Depends ⚖️

**Reason:** Acceptance varies between cultures and organizations.

---

## 8. Hedonism (اللذة)

**Core Idea:** Pleasure is the highest good and pain should be minimized.

### Case: Using a Convenient Mobile App
Users share personal data to access a highly enjoyable service.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** The pleasure and convenience are valued.

### Case: Choosing Entertainment Over Work
A person chooses leisure because it brings greater enjoyment.

**Verdict:** Not Guilty ✅

**Reason:** Pleasure is considered the ultimate good.

---

## 9. Emotivism (العاطفية)

**Core Idea:** Moral statements express feelings rather than objective truths.

### Case: AI Replacing Human Customer Service
Some people say it is wrong because they dislike talking to machines.

**Verdict:** Depends ⚖️

**Reason:** The judgment reflects emotions, not objective facts.

### Case: Gun Restrictions
Someone says "Gun restrictions are bad."

**Verdict:** Depends ⚖️

**Reason:** The statement expresses a personal feeling rather than a universal moral truth.
